About

+

Understory Films was founded in 2021 by Sepehr Samimi and Jen Muranetz and is dedicated to making award-winning, impactful, and story-driven documentaries and narrative films. We are based on the unceded and ancestral territories of the Musqueam, Squamish and Tsleil-Waututh First Nations, known as Vancouver, BC.

The phrase "Understory" refers to the layers of plants and undergrowth covering a forest floor. Mirroring the natural world, our films dive below the canopy to create meaningful, critical, and thought-provoking subjects, with an aim to spark discussion and shift perspectives on critical social, environmental, and political issues of our time.

Our works have screened in film festivals worldwide. We've been supported by Telefilm Canada, Creative BC, Canada Council for the Arts, BC Arts Council, and the National Film Board of Canada. Our team has participated in programs such as Hot Docs Accelerator, Canadian Creative Accelerator, DOC NYC Voices of Canada, and Whistler Docs Lab.

Work

Judas Tree

2027 | Scripted Feature | In Pre-Production

A political psychological drama following an Iranian-Canadian woman whose quiet life is upended when she must decide whether to testify against her former interrogator, confronting the haunting consequences of exile, justice, and silence.

Director: Susan Bayani
Producers: Sepehr Samimi & Nach Dudsdeemaytha
Funded by Telefilm Canada, Creative BC
Produced in conjunction with Musubi Arts and Scythia Films

Light Through the Blindfold

2025 | 25 Minutes | Documentary Short

An Iranian-Canadian goalball athlete with only five percent vision is offered a life-changing surgery. As the possibility of seeing again stirs old memories and new dreams, she must confront what sight truly means to her.

Written & Directed by: Alireza Kazemipour
Produced by: Sina Nazarian & Sepehr Samimi
Director of Photography: Sepehr Samimi
World & International Premiere: 2025

Fairy Creek

2024 | 86 Minutes | Documentary Feature

A gripping frontline dive into the lives of activists urgently blocking logging roads on Vancouver Island, Canada, in a last-ditch attempt to stop old-growth logging in the pristine Fairy Creek valley.

Director: Jen Muranetz
Producers: Jen Muranetz & Sepehr Samimi
Executive Producers: Mark Achbar, Mitchell Steinke, Neal Livingston

Abundance

2023 | 29 Minutes | Documentary Short

An ancient Persian poem becomes the inspiration for a community on a path of personal growth, healing, and better relationship to the earth. A collection of cinematic portraits exploring the people living at Abundance Community Farm near Vancouver, BC.

A film by: Sepehr Samimi
